Pulmonary Function Tests (PFTs)▲

1. Volume Parameters:
Tidal Volume (VT or TV) → The amount of air inhaled or exhaled in a normal breath (~500 mL in adults).
Inspiratory Reserve Volume (IRV) → Extra air that can be inhaled after a normal inspiration (~3000 mL).
Inspiratory Capacity: TV + IRV ~ 3.500 ml
Expiratory Reserve Volume (ERV) → Extra air that can be exhaled after a normal expiration (~1100 mL).
Residual Volume (RV) → Air remaining in the lungs after a full exhalation (~1200 mL in adults).
Total Lung Capacity (TLC) → The maximum volume of air the lungs can hold.
VC (Vital Capacity) → The maximum amount of air a person can exhale after a full inhalation. It represents the total volume of air that can be moved in and out of the lungs voluntarily.
Functional Residual Capacity (FRC) → ~2,300 mL Air remaining in the lungs after passive exhalation.
Dead space air (150 mL): This part never reaches the alveoli and remains in the airways. When you exhale, it's the first to leave.
Alveolar air (~350 mL): This portion reaches the alveoli, participates in gas exchange, and mixes with the already existing air in the Functional Residual Capacity (FRC), which is the air remaining after passive exhalation.

VC = TV + IRV + ERV
TLC = VC + RV

2. Flow and Capacity Parameters:
Forced Vital Capacity (FVC) → Maximum air exhaled after a full inspiration.
Forced Expiratory Volume in 1 Second (FEV₁) → Air volume exhaled in the first second of an FVC maneuver.
FEV₁/FVC Ratio → Percentage of FVC exhaled in the first second; helps diagnose obstructive diseases.
Peak Expiratory Flow (PEF or PEFR) → Maximum speed of exhalation.

3. Additional Parameters:
Forced Expiratory Flow (FEF 25-75%) → Measures flow rate between 25% and 75% of FVC; sensitive to small airway disease.
Inspiratory Capacity (IC) → Maximum air that can be inspired after normal exhalation:

IC=VT+IRV

Functional Residual Capacity (FRC) → Air left in lungs after normal exhalation:

FRC=ERV+RV

Minute Ventilation (VE) → Total air volume inhaled/exhaled per minute:

VE=VT×RespiratoryRate

Clinical Relevance:
Obstructive diseases (e.g., COPD, asthma): ↓ FEV₁, ↓ FEV₁/FVC ratio.
Restrictive diseases (e.g., fibrosis): ↓ TLC, ↓ FVC, but normal or high FEV₁/FVC.

1. Spirometry Tests (Basic Lung Function)
Forced Vital Capacity (FVC) → Maximum air exhaled after deep inhalation.
Forced Expiratory Volume in 1 second (FEV₁) → Air exhaled in the first second of FVC.
FEV₁/FVC Ratio → Used to diagnose obstructive lung diseases.
Peak Expiratory Flow (PEF or PEFR) → Fastest speed of exhalation.
Forced Expiratory Flow (FEF 25-75%) → Assesses small airway function.

2. Lung Volume Tests (Static Lung Volumes)
Total Lung Capacity (TLC) → Maximum air in lungs after full inhalation.
Vital Capacity (VC) → Maximum air exhaled after full inhalation.
Residual Volume (RV) → Air remaining in lungs after full exhalation.
Functional Residual Capacity (FRC) → Air left in lungs after normal exhalation.
Inspiratory Capacity (IC) → Maximum air inhaled after normal exhalation.
(Measured with spirometry, helium dilution, nitrogen washout, or body plethysmography.)
3. Gas Exchange and Diffusion Tests
Diffusing Capacity of the Lungs for Carbon Monoxide (DLCO or TLCO) → Measures how well gases move from the lungs to the blood.
Alveolar-Arterial Oxygen Gradient (A-a Gradient) → Assesses oxygen transfer efficiency.
(Used for interstitial lung disease, pulmonary fibrosis, and emphysema.)

4. Bronchodilator Reversibility Test
Spirometry before and after a bronchodilator (e.g., albuterol/salbutamol).
Helps distinguish asthma (reversible obstruction) from COPD (non-reversible obstruction).
5. Bronchial Provocation Test
Methacholine or Histamine Challenge Test → Evaluates airway hyperresponsiveness in asthma.
Exercise-Induced Bronchoconstriction Test → Assesses exercise-related breathing issues.

6. Maximum Respiratory Pressure Tests
Maximal Inspiratory Pressure (MIP or PIMAX) → Measures diaphragm and inspiratory muscle strength.
Maximal Expiratory Pressure (MEP or PEMAX) → Measures expiratory muscle strength.
(Used for neuromuscular diseases like ALS, muscular dystrophy.)

7. Cardiopulmonary Exercise Testing (CPET)
Measures oxygen uptake (VO₂ max), carbon dioxide output, and ventilation during exercise.
Used for evaluating dyspnea (shortness of breath), heart/lung conditions, and fitness levels.
8. Pulse Oximetry & Arterial Blood Gas (ABG)
Pulse Oximetry (SpO₂) → Estimates oxygen saturation in blood.
Arterial Blood Gas (ABG) → Measures oxygen (PaO₂), carbon dioxide (PaCO₂), pH, and bicarbonate levels.

8. VCO2: CO₂ production = ~200 mL/min (CO₂ exhaled/min)
