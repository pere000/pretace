    19	In Aquinas’ philosophy, prime matter is matter itself and pure potentiality to form; it cannot exist independently and requires a substantial form to be actualized. Matter and form together constitute a material substance, with individuation arising from designated matter rather than from form. Angels are immaterial, complete substances composed of form alone, while the human soul is an immaterial, subsistent form that is naturally ordered to the body and incomplete without it. Principles and ideas are not substances but forms or relations existing in intellect, and fictional entities such as unicorns are beings of reason, not real substances.<br><br>
