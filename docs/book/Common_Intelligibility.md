# TACE: Seeking a Common Intelligibility

TACE (Theory of Actualized Conscious Experience) seeks a common
intelligibility capable of integrating knowledge originating from
different scientific, philosophical, theological, and cultural
traditions without reducing one tradition to another. Rather than
assuming equivalence between traditions, TACE searches for
constitutional invariants that remain stable under different historical
and linguistic expressions.

## Principle of Constitutional Convergence

Independent cultural, philosophical, ethical, and religious traditions
may exhibit structurally isomorphic conceptions that indicate the
presence of underlying constitutional invariants. Such convergence
constitutes evidence for investigation but not, by itself, proof of
constitutional validity.

Within TACE, every candidate invariant must undergo constitutional
evaluation before being admitted into the appropriate constitutional or
domain ontology. The canonical ontology therefore grows through
constitutional validation rather than textual accumulation.

This principle explains why TACE can legitimately investigate
historically independent traditions without assuming that they are
equivalent. Their agreement may indicate a deeper constitutional
structure whose validity must be demonstrated through the constitutional
framework.

## Constitutional Compatibility

Constitutional Compatibility is the **formal evaluation performed by the
Constitutional Reasoner** to determine whether an imported
constitutional module, domain module, concept, proposition, relation, or
inference structure may participate in the currently active
constitutional framework.

Compatibility is evaluated according to the constitutional primitives,
archetypes, principles, axioms, inference rules, and admissibility
criteria defined by the active constitutional ontology.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Constitutional Principle of Archetypal Sufficiency

<div class="definition">

Every ontologically admissible entity, property, relation, or process
must be constitutionally expressible in terms of the Archetype Triad:

- Being
- Subsisting Consciousness
- Structural Form

No candidate concept that cannot be reduced to, or consistently derived
from, these constitutional archetypes may be admitted into the
constitutional framework.

</div>

## Native Constitutional Modules

The Constitutional Reasoner is ontology-independent and can operate over
any constitutionally structured ontology.

Consequently, the native constitutional modules (**Thomas_Aquinas**,
**TACE**, **Maturana_Varela**, and **Mystici_Corporis_Christi**) are not
strict runtime dependencies. Alternative constitutional modules may
therefore be imported and evaluated.

However, the present implementation has been designed, developed, and
validated using the native constitutional modules. Although alternative
constitutional frameworks may be loaded, the current application does
not guarantee correct operation unless the certified native
constitutional framework is employed.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Constitutional AI Integration

**External knowledge may be incorporated as retrieval sources within the
TACE semantic framework**. Retrieval serves only to identify candidate
concepts, propositions, symbolic structures, and semantic relations.
Retrieval never modifies the ontology.

Candidate knowledge is evaluated by the Constitutional Reasoner.
Constitutional modules are evaluated for compatibility with the active
constitutional framework. Domain modules are evaluated to determine
whether their concepts and relations are both constitutionally
admissible and semantically relevant to the currently loaded semantic
corpus.

The outcome of this process is not a textual summary but a
constitutional compatibility assessment that guides the admission,
qualification, rejection, or mapping of imported knowledge.

<div class="note">

**Fundamental Principle**

TACE does not evaluate whether something exists. TACE evaluates whether
something can exist according to the active constitutional ontology.

</div>

The Archetype Triad is not a collection of categories into which
existing objects are classified. Rather, it constitutes the
constitutional conditions of ontological intelligibility. Every
admissible ontological entity, relation, property, or process must be
constitutionally expressible through these archetypes or be consistently
derivable from them.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Module Admission

**When a module is imported, TACE performs constitutional compatibility
evaluation before the module becomes active.**

For constitutional modules, the evaluation determines whether their
primitives, principles, archetypes, axioms, and inference structures are
compatible with the active constitutional framework.

For domain modules, the evaluation determines whether their concepts and
relations are constitutionally admissible and semantically relevant to
the currently active semantic corpus.

### Compatibility Classification

- **Certified Compatible**  
  Fully satisfies the constitutional assumptions used during the
  development and validation of the application.
- **Compatible with Reservations**  
  Operationally usable but requiring constitutional mappings, warnings,
  or restricted reasoning.
- **Partially Compatible**  
  Only a subset of the module satisfies constitutional admissibility.
- **Incompatible**  
  Fundamental constitutional conflicts prevent admission into the active
  constitutional framework.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Constitutional Evaluation versus Ontology Growth

Constitutional evaluation does not imply that every admissible concept
becomes part of the canonical constitutional ontology.

An admissible concept may instead become part of a domain ontology
associated with the currently loaded semantic corpus.

For example, an imported module describing fly biology may contain
concepts that are constitutionally admissible while remaining entirely
specific to that domain. Their admission enriches the fly biology
ontology without modifying the constitutional foundations of TACE.

## Illustrative Example

> aSDF SAFDA DSAFG

The symbolic sequence above unquestionably exists as a sequence of
characters. It may represent random data, an encrypted message, or a
valid symbolic code. Its existence is therefore not questioned.

Nevertheless, the Constitutional Reasoner has no basis for admitting it
into the currently loaded semantic corpus because its constitutional
significance and semantic relevance remain undetermined.

TACE therefore distinguishes between the existence of a symbolic object
and its constitutional admissibility within the active knowledge
framework.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Conclusion

TACE is neither a textual summarizer nor a repository of accumulated
knowledge. It is a constitutional framework for evaluating whether
imported knowledge can participate coherently within an active
ontological system. Retrieval provides candidate knowledge;
constitutional reasoning determines admissibility; ontology growth
results from constitutionally validated integration rather than from
unrestricted accumulation.
