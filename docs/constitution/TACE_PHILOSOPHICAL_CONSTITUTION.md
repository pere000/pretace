# TACE Philosophical Constitution

**Status**

Canonical

**Authority**

This constitution is subordinate to accepted ontology and accepted ADRs.

---

# 1. Purpose

1.1. This constitution SHALL define the canonical ontological commitments of TACE.

1.2. This constitution SHALL answer what is true according to TACE at the level of ontology.

1.3. This constitution SHALL remain implementation-independent.

# 2. Scope

2.1. This constitution SHALL concern only ontology and metaphysics.

2.2. This constitution SHALL NOT contain software architecture, semantic reasoning rules, repository organization, or implementation procedures.

2.3. This constitution SHALL use only accepted TACE ontology and accepted ADRs.

# 3. Canonical Ontological Commitments

3.1. TACE SHALL affirm Logos.

3.2. TACE SHALL affirm Creation.

3.3. TACE SHALL affirm Prime Potency.

3.4. TACE SHALL affirm the Matrix.

3.5. TACE SHALL affirm λ (Q-Form).

3.6. TACE SHALL affirm Forms.

3.7. TACE SHALL affirm Actualization.

3.8. TACE SHALL affirm Participation.

3.9. TACE SHALL affirm the Silver Bridge.

3.10. TACE SHALL affirm Consciousness.

3.11. TACE SHALL affirm the Human Soul.

3.12. TACE SHALL affirm Angels.

3.13. TACE SHALL affirm Reality.

3.14. TACE SHALL affirm the Whole.

3.15. TACE SHALL affirm Universal Actualization.

3.16. TACE SHALL affirm Formally Transparent Participation.

3.17. TACE SHALL affirm Topologically Organized Participation.

3.18. TACE SHALL affirm Formal Transparency.

3.19. TACE SHALL affirm Ontological Transparency.

3.20. TACE SHALL affirm Epistemological Transparency.

3.21. TACE SHALL affirm Subsistent Formal Substances.

3.22. TACE SHALL affirm Structural Forms.

3.23. TACE SHALL affirm the Kantian Noumenon as ontological reference.

3.24. TACE SHALL define the Silver Bridge as the Universal Onto-Epistemological Isomorphic Principle of Creation through which the Realm of Forms and the Physical Realm remain intrinsically and inextricably interlocked while preserving lawful isomorphic correspondence without reduction.

# 4. Foundational Axioms

4.1. The Logos SHALL be the unique uncreated Creator.

4.2. Prime Potency SHALL be the first created possibility.

4.3. Every created possibility SHALL depend upon the Logos.

4.4. The Matrix SHALL be created.

4.5. λ (Q-Form) SHALL be created.

4.6. Creation SHALL precede every created possibility.

4.7. Reality SHALL unfold through historical actualization.

4.8. Ontological superposition SHALL ground quantum superposition.

4.9. Human souls SHALL be created historically at conception.

4.10. Nothing SHALL exist outside the Whole.

4.11. Actualization SHALL be universal and SHALL NOT be restricted to the Physical Realm.

4.12. Created reality SHALL comprise two complementary and inextricably interlocked domains: the Realm of Forms and the Physical Realm.

4.13. Creation and Actualization SHALL be ontologically distinct operations.

4.14. TACE SHALL distinguish an accepted ontological framework of Primordial Creation, Historical Creation, and Continuous Actualization.

4.15. Human souls SHALL belong to Historical Creation.

4.16. Angels SHALL belong to Primordial Creation.

# 5. Fundamental Ontological Principles

5.1. Being SHALL be understood through the ordered relation of Creator, creation, potency, form, and actualization.

5.2. Created being SHALL participate in the Whole.

5.3. Created being SHALL receive intelligibility through form.

5.4. Actualization SHALL be the passage from potency to determinate being.

5.5. Participation SHALL express the ordered dependence of created being upon the Logos.

5.6. The Matrix SHALL be a created ontological order.

5.7. λ (Q-Form) SHALL be a created formal order within TACE ontology.

5.8. Domain differentiation SHALL be determined by participation mode.

5.9. Formally Transparent Participation SHALL denote non-obstructive participation of instantiated Form.

5.10. Topologically Organized Participation SHALL denote participation through organized Q-Substances.

5.11. Structural Forms SHALL participate through actualization.

5.12. Subsistent Formal Substances and Structural Forms SHALL remain ontologically distinct categories.

# 6. Canonical Relations Among First Principles

6.1. The Logos SHALL stand as the source of all created being.

6.2. Prime Potency SHALL arise within creation and SHALL NOT be uncreated.

6.3. Forms SHALL be intelligible principles of determination.

6.4. Actualization SHALL realize what is possible in creation.

6.5. Participation SHALL relate creatures to the Whole without collapsing creature into Creator.

6.6. The Silver Bridge SHALL relate consciousness to the ordered reality of the Whole.

6.7. Ontological superposition SHALL be prior to quantum superposition in the canonical order of explanation.

6.8. Q-Form SHALL be the universal principle of instantiation.

6.9. Q-Form SHALL NOT be identified with Creation.

6.10. Ontological superposition SHALL ground quantum superposition.

6.11. The Silver Bridge SHALL constitute the first-principle onto-epistemological isomorphic relation through which both domains remain lawfully interlocked within one created Whole.

# 7. Anthropological Commitments

7.1. The Human Soul SHALL be created.

7.2. The Human Soul SHALL be historically created at conception.

7.3. Consciousness SHALL be a real ontological datum in TACE.

7.4. The human being SHALL be ordered through participation in reality and the Whole.

7.5. The Silver Bridge SHALL articulate the relation of consciousness to the ontological order.

7.6. At human conception, the creation of an individual human soul and the historical emergence of embodied human participation SHALL be treated as one ontological beginning of the human being.

# 8. Angelic Commitments

8.1. Angels SHALL be real created beings.

8.2. Angels SHALL NOT be uncreated.

8.3. Angels SHALL belong to the created order while differing from human beings in mode of being.

8.4. Angels SHALL participate in the Whole according to their proper created nature.

8.5. Angels SHALL be classified under Primordial Creation.

8.6. Angels and Human Souls SHALL NOT be conflated as the same ontological mode of created being.

# 9. Reality and the Whole

9.1. Reality SHALL be ordered as a Whole.

9.2. The Whole SHALL include all that exists.

9.3. Nothing SHALL exist outside the Whole.

9.4. Created beings SHALL be intelligible only within the Whole.

9.5. The canonical order of reality SHALL preserve distinction between Creator and creation.

9.6. The Kantian Noumenon SHALL NOT be treated as a separate ontological realm.

9.7. The Kantian Noumenon SHALL NOT be treated as actualized ontology.

9.8. TACE SHALL treat ultimate noumenal constitution as undetermined within this constitution.
