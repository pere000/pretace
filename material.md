<code>**Nothing is absolutely motionless**. A body said to be “at rest” is never truly still; rather, its *state of rest corresponds to a dynamic equilibrium in which underlying motions persist as minimal fluctuations*—**a trembling state produced by the superposition of relational states, characteristic of an object embedded within multiple tensorial structures whose balanced vectorialities constrain motion to an infinitesimal neighborhood of rest**. Therefore, **Q-form is actualized in its raw constitution through certain forms</code>—**namely raw Q-pseudomaterial substances**—<code>with space-time force vectoriality**—that is, the potency to be at rest or in motion, thereby enabling their actualization as physical configurations within space-time, while other forms remain purely immaterial. In the domain of sole forms, there is no space, time, or force.</code>

 [In TACE](https://pere000.github.io/tace/), **<code>material objects</code>** are of **immaterial** nature, same as objects sole forms though of distinct category. Theese entities, capable of being **<code>apprehended through sensors or sensorial devices</code>**, and appropriate processors—such as the human neuropsyche—are, prior to transduction, called **<code>raw Q-pseudomaterial substances</code>** or *raw signals* of various kinds. Once they reach sensorial systems, they are transduced into **constrained signals** and are then called **<code>Q-pseudomaterial signifiers</code>**. Following Saussure, *every signifier necessarily carries a signified*, which in TACE corresponds to meanings and qualia that may be actualized in consciousness—that is, through the **morphing faculty granted to human intelligence**, by which meanings, qualia, or both are associated with morphing structures or imagery in sensu lato, such as visual, acoustic, and other **modes of sensorial apprehension**.


A **material object** is that which **can be sensed or measured** and is never “directly given” by the actualized Q-form of any Q-pseudomaterial substance—or, at the minimal level, a **Q-substance**. There are also substances that exist solely as forms—such as souls, angels, spirits, ideas, and structural forms in general—which do not possess a **Q-form substrate**, as they belong to a distinct immaterial teleological category, separate from both the **form and Q-form categories**.

 Olfactory, gustatory, tactile, and thermal signals can give rise to corresponding imagery, allowing the neuropsyche to associate these sensory modalities with dynamic, **morphing structures** 
 in consciousness—that is, mental images (***consciousness-neuropsyche looping***) that continuously fluctuate or ***oscillate subliminally among multiple possibilities***, tending to anticipate forthcoming contents. Through multisensory integration—for example, viewing food evoking mental representations that trigger salivation—these ***signals can be actualized as qualia or experiential meaning***.

 Olfactory, gustatory, tactile, and thermal inputs are described here as *capable of producing corresponding mental imagery*: when the senses register smells, tastes, textures, or temperature, the brain can construct internal representations that resemble those sensations. Calling these representations “dynamic, ‘morphing’ structures” emphasizes that they are not fixed snapshots but *fluid patterns in consciousness that change over time as the brain continuously updates its predictions and interpretations*.

The phrase “consciousness–neuropsyche looping” points to a *feedback loop between neural processing and conscious experience*: perceptual circuitry generates candidate images or hypotheses, those images shape attention and expectation, and incoming sensory data then confirm or revise them. The claim that these images “continuously fluctuate or oscillate subliminally among multiple possibilities” maps onto predictive or probabilistic models of perception, where the mind holds multiple potential interpretations and *tends to anticipate likely future inputs rather than waiting passively*.

*Through multisensory integration*, signals from different modalities combine to make those representations more vivid and behaviorally effective. The example—seeing food evoking a mental representation that triggers salivation—illustrates how an *internally generated image can produce physiological and behavioral responses*. Framing the result as qualia or “experiential meaning” highlights that when neural patterns cohere across modalities and processing levels, they *become felt, meaningful experiences rather than mere raw signals*.
