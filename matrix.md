In cosmology and the philosophy of science, the [anthropic principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthropic_principle)—commonly formulated as an observation-selection effect—maintains that any observation of the universe is conditioned by the biological and physical prerequisites necessary for observers to arise. The [TACE framework](https://idealistic-mosquito.static.domains/introduction.html), by contrast, situates the limit of knowledge not in the contingent conditions of observers, but in the **intrinsic relational structure of the universe itself**. Reality is constituted as a **web of interdependent parts**, each existing through dynamic relations with the whole. Consequently, knowledge that lies beyond the scope of direct sensory observation remains accessible through [intellectual inference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inference), by virtue of the _isomorphic participation of all intelligences in the structural network of being itself_. That **web of interdependent parts and intrinsic relational structure of the universe** is what, in TACE, is precisely named **[Matrix](https://diaspora.psyco.fr/tags/trondiasporapsycofr_1764285169801)**.
