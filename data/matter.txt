Without intelligibility, the self-evident “Cogito, ergo sum” could not be conceived. But is intelligibility necessary for something to exist? Is the preexistence of intelligence necessary for any other object to exist?

Intelligibility is the capacity of an object to be apprehended by intellectuality (the subject’s power of intellectual apprehension), grounded in both 1st) its intrinsic form and 2nd) its potential to relate to and interact with other forms. If an object has zero relational potential (2nd = 0) while its intrinsic form exists (1st = 1), it is practically irrelevant: it affects no events, is meaningless, and non-functional. Within the universe, no event requires such a feature; consequently, it will never be instantiated by the Matrix, except as a chimera.

A. Is intelligibility necessary for something to exist? — No
B. Is the preexistence of intelligence necessary for any other object to exist? — No

	Even if A and B are true, every event occurs in one of two ways:

		Necessarily, by existing on its own through intrinsic power, or
		Contingently, through an external cause (extrinsic power).

	In the case of intrinsic, unconditioned power:

		An uncaused universe is possible only if it exists necessarily;
		otherwise, events require causes. Scientifically, since the universe
		appears to have a high probability of a beginning (t = 0), it requires a
		cause.

		Consequently, as the universe cannot be self-caused—because for A to
		cause B, A must exist prior to B in temporal terms, we must conclude
		that the universe has an external cause, observer, or designer.


Thomistic / classical metaphysical view — e.g., Thomas Aquinas:

	Things exist independently of human minds, but their intelligibility comes from
	an ultimate intellect (God). Reality is intelligible because it is grounded in
	divine intelligence.
	
	First Cause — Primary Intelligence (source of intellectuality):
	non-imprinted, pure intelligibility, self-subsisting and foundational.

	Humans — Secondary intelligence (image of intellectuality):
	imprinted intellect + free intentionality + qualia + imprinting power,
	i.e., participatory intelligence expressed through lived experience.

	•  AI — imprinted, no qualia, algoritmic-stocastic (non intentional), no
	   imprinting power (structured output without intelligence or experience)
	•  Natural processes — imprinted, no qualia, non intentional, no imprinting
	   power (form transmission, not creation)
