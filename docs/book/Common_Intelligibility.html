TACE: Seeking a Common Intelligibility

Principle of Constitutional Convergence

Independent cultural, philosophical, ethical, and religious traditions may exhibit structurally isomorphic conceptions that indicate the presence of underlying constitutional invariants. Such convergence constitutes evidence for investigation but not, by itself, proof of constitutional validity. Within TACE, every candidate invariant must undergo constitutional evaluation before it can be admitted into the canonical ontology.

This principle explains why TACE can legitimately study historically unrelated traditions without assuming they are equivalent. It provides a formal methodology for ontology growth: concepts are admitted because they survive constitutional evaluation. TACE therefore functions as a constitutional discovery framework whose ontology is the canonical result of discovering, testing, and integrating constitutional invariants.

Constitutional AI Integration

External knowledge may be incorporated as retrieval sources within the TACE semantic framework. Retrieval serves only to identify candidate concepts, propositions, and relational structures. These candidates are subsequently subjected to constitutional evaluation by the Constitutional Reasoner. Constitutional modules are evaluated for compatibility with the active constitutional framework. Domain modules are evaluated to determine whether their concepts and relations are constitutionally admissible and relevant to the currently loaded semantic corpus. The outcome is not a textual summary but a constitutional compatibility assessment that guides the admission, rejection, or qualification of imported knowledge.

TACE does not evaluate whether something exists. TACE evaluates whether something can exist according to the constitutional ontology.

The triad is not a set of categories into which existing things are classified. Rather, it constitutes the constitutional conditions of intelligibility. Anything proposed as an ontological entity must be constitutionally expressible in terms of the triad. If it cannot be so expressed, it is not admitted into the ontology—not because it has been disproved empirically, but because it lacks constitutional intelligibility.

Constitutional Principle of Archetypal Sufficiency

Every ontologically admissible entity, property, relation, or process must be constitutionally expressible in terms of the Archetype Triad of Being, Subsisting Consciousness, and Structural Form. No candidate concept that cannot be reduced to, or derived from, these constitutional archetypes may be incorporated into the canonical ontology.

Native Constitutional Modules

The Constitutional Reasoner is ontology-independent and can operate over any constitutionally structured ontology. The native constitutional modules (Thomas_Aquinas, TACE, Maturana_Varela, and Mystici_Corporis_Christi) are therefore not strict runtime dependencies. However, the present implementation has been designed, developed, and validated using these modules. Alternative constitutional modules may be loaded and evaluated for constitutional compatibility, but the current application does not guarantee correct operation unless the certified native constitutional framework is used.

However, the architecture, algorithms, validation procedures, and inference mechanisms of the current implementation have been developed, tested, and verified using the native constitutional modules. Therefore, although the application may continue to operate when alternative constitutional modules are loaded, the current design does not guarantee the correctness, completeness, or consistency of its reasoning under those conditions.

Constitutional certification:

When a module is imported, TACE performs a constitutional compatibility evaluation. Constitutional modules are assessed to determine whether their primitives, principles, and inference structures are compatible with the active constitutional framework. Domain modules are evaluated to ensure that their concepts and relations are constitutionally admissible within the loaded constitutional ontology.

Certified Constitutional Modules — modules for which the application guarantees correct operation because they satisfy the constitutional assumptions used during development.
Compatible Constitutional Modules — modules that the engine can load and reason over, but whose behavior has not been formally verified.

Certified Compatible – fully satisfies the constitutional requirements.
Compatible with Reservations – usable, but some concepts require mapping or generate warnings.
Partially Compatible – only a subset of the module is constitutionally admissible.
Incompatible – fundamental constitutional conflicts prevent admission.

Domain Modules — semantic knowledge modules interpreted through the currently active constitutional ontology.
