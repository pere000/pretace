## TACE and the Copenhagen Interpretation

Although TACE accepts the empirical predictions of quantum mechanics, it differs fundamentally from the Copenhagen interpretation at the ontological level.

The Copenhagen interpretation is primarily an **interpretation of quantum theory**. It treats the wave function as the mathematical object used to predict measurement outcomes and generally refrains from asserting what physically exists prior to measurement. Measurement is taken as a fundamental process that produces a definite outcome, while the ontology underlying that transition remains intentionally unspecified.

TACE, by contrast, is **a formal ontology of reality** rather than an interpretation of quantum mechanics. Quantum mechanics occupies only the Physical Level of the framework. The wave function is understood as the mathematical representation of the current physical state, whereas the underlying ontological reality is constituted by the Matrix, the Q-Form, physical substances, and the lawful process of actualization.

Within TACE, physical actualization is the fundamental ontological process. Every physical interaction that produces an actual event transforms the structured potentiality of the Q-Form into a new physical state (`q-form₁`). Conscious observation does not create this transformation. Instead, through the Silver Bridge, consciousness establishes an intentional correspondence with the already actualized physical reality.

Consequently, measurement is not ontologically privileged. It is simply one particular instance of lawful physical actualization. The same ontological principles govern every actualization, whether or not a conscious observer is present.

Accordingly, TACE does not interpret the transition from `q-form₀` to `q-form₁` as a "collapse" of reality. Rather, it understands it as the lawful ontological actualization of structured potentiality under the constraints imposed by the Matrix. The wave function describes this transition mathematically, but it does not itself constitute the ontological reality.

Thus, the principal difference between Copenhagen and TACE is not mathematical but ontological. Copenhagen begins with the formalism of quantum mechanics and interprets measurement. TACE begins with a comprehensive ontology of being and regards quantum mechanics as one physical description within that broader ontological framework.

> **Copenhagen treats measurement as fundamental within the interpretation of quantum mechanics. TACE treats lawful ontological actualization as fundamental and regards measurement as one particular instance of physical actualization within a broader ontological structure.**

That single sentence captures the essential distinction. The Theory of Actualized Conscious Experience (TACE) is a formal ontological framework developed to describe the structure of reality, consciousness, and actualization. It combines Aristotelian–Thomistic metaphysics with contemporary mathematical and physical insights into a coherent ontology intended to support both philosophical reasoning and AI-assisted semantic reasoning.

TACE distinguishes between the Physical Realm, in which material substances exist under space-time locality, and the Realm of Forms, in which intelligible Forms are present as the objects of acts of consciousness. These realms are not independent realities but complementary ontological modes of one created order.

The Creator is the necessary source of all being and the ground of every actualization. Creation unfolds according to a lawful formal order called the Matrix, which determines the admissible possibilities through which created reality may become actual. The Matrix is not conscious, intentional, or causal in itself; it is the universal formal order governing lawful actualization.

Within the Physical Realm, Q-Form represents structured ontological potentiality. It does not determine a single future but the space of admissible actualizations available to created substances. Actualization selects one possibility while preserving the lawful order established by the Matrix.

The Silver Bridge is the universal onto-epistemological principle that mediates every lawful transition between the Physical Realm and the Realm of Forms. In conscious beings, it enables intentional knowledge by allowing physical neuropsychic processes and intelligible Forms to correspond without reducing one to the other. It also governs the embodiment of intentional acts into physical realization.

Consciousness is understood as the substantial activity of the soul through acts such as intellect, intentionality, and will. Acts of consciousness actualize intelligible Forms as meaningful objects while preserving the distinction between the subject, the object, and their respective ontological modes.

TACE is intended as a formal ontology rather than a speculative philosophy. Its concepts are defined canonically, organized into ontology modules, and related through explicit ontological relations. This structure allows the ontology to serve as the semantic foundation for AI-assisted reasoning, canonical document generation, and the systematic investigation of questions concerning consciousness, meaning, knowledge, and reality.
